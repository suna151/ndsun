What is Genome Editing?


What is Gene Editing? Gene editing, also known as genome editing, is a way of making direct and specific changes to an organism's DNA or cells. An enzyme, known as CRISPR, cuts the DNA at a specific sequence. When the cut is repaired by the cell, this is known as an edit to the sequence, resulting in gene editing.

Systems Map

The first step of this project was to research the history and different facets of gene editing. Thus, I first looked at the origins of gene editing and the technology behind what makes modern gene editing possible. Afterwards, I then analyzed the specific points of historical impact of gene editing through the lens of modern medicine. Finally, I made sure to research the societal influences that gene editing has over the general public as well as the public opinion towards gene editing. The research that went in to this project was then synthesized into a map of the gene editing system, visualizing nodes that are inevitably intertwined and culminate into the modern understanding of gene editing.

Predictive Product

The second step to this project was to design a future product that could exist within the realm of gene editing. Thus, CRISPR EVE was born. CRISPR EVE is a human specific enzyme that allows for scientists to gene edit human DNA directly at a much more efficient rate. Unlike CRISPR cas9 and CRISPR cpf1, CRISPR EVE is a product specifically designs for human genome engineering and alterations. This can range from stopping HIV or cancer in the embryonic stages of life to even altering physical features such as eye color or height. I took a graphic of a standard CRISPR diagram and reiterated to fit CRISPR EVE. Instead of using guide RNA, CRISPR EVE only utilizes genomic scissors, which streamlines the process. This opens the door to a more efficient and cheaper gene editing, thus increasing the accessibility of gene editing exponentially.
